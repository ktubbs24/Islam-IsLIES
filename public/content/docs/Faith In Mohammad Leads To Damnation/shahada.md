# The Shahada - The Islamic Declaration of Faith

The Shahada is the Islamic declaration of faith and the first of the Five Pillars of Islam. It states: "There is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is the messenger of Allah" (in Arabic: "La ilaha illa Allah, Muhammad rasul Allah").

This simple statement forms the foundation of Islamic belief and practice, but from a Christian perspective, it contains fundamental errors that lead away from true salvation.

## The Two Parts of the Shahada

### "There is no god but Allah"

This first part affirms strict monotheism (tawhid) and rejects all other gods or concepts of God that differ from the Islamic understanding of Allah. While Christians also believe in one God, the Islamic concept of Allah rejects:

1. **The Trinity**: Islam views the Christian doctrine of the Trinity as polytheism (shirk), claiming Christians worship three gods rather than one. This misunderstands the Christian belief that God is one Being in three Persons.

2. **God as Father**: Islam rejects the notion that God can be a father, particularly to Jesus. The Quran states, "Allah begets not, nor is He begotten" (Surah 112:3), directly contradicting Jesus' teaching about God as Father.

3. **God's Nature**: The Biblical God is revealed as inherently relational, loving, and personally involved with humanity. The Islamic Allah is often portrayed as more distant, primarily concerned with submission.

### "Muhammad is the messenger of Allah"

This second part affirms Muhammad as Allah's final and greatest prophet. This claim:

1. **Places Muhammad above Jesus**: Islam teaches that Muhammad is the final and superior prophet, effectively ranking him above Jesus Christ, whom Christians recognize as God incarnate.

2. **Makes Muhammad's Teaching Authoritative**: Accepting the Shahada means accepting Muhammad's teachings as divine revelation, despite numerous contradictions with Biblical Christianity.

3. **Rejects Jesus' Exclusive Claims**: Jesus said, "I am the way, and the truth, and the life. No one comes to the Father except through me" (John 14:6). The Shahada substitutes Muhammad's way for Jesus' way.

## The Shahada and Salvation

From a Christian perspective, the Shahada leads away from salvation because:

1. **It Rejects Jesus' Divinity**: By affirming that Allah has no partners or equals, the Shahada implicitly denies that Jesus is God, contradicting Jesus' own claims and the foundation of Christian faith.

2. **It Replaces Faith in Christ**: True salvation comes through faith in Jesus Christ alone, not through adherence to religious formulas or human prophets.

3. **It Begins a Path of Works-Righteousness**: The Shahada is just the first of the Five Pillars of Islam, starting Muslims on a path of earning salvation through works rather than receiving it by grace through faith.

4. **It Denies the Trinity**: By rejecting the Trinity, the Shahada denies the true nature of God as revealed in Scripture and the means by which God saves humanity through the Son.

## The Biblical Alternative

In contrast to the Shahada, the Bible teaches:

> "If you confess with your mouth that Jesus is Lord and believe in your heart that God raised him from the dead, you will be saved." - Romans 10:9

This confession affirms Jesus' divinity, His lordship, His death for our sins, and His resurrection—the core of the gospel message. It acknowledges that salvation comes through [[Jesus]] Christ alone, not through [[Mohammad]] or adherence to Islamic law.

Rather than reciting the Shahada, true salvation comes through placing your faith in Jesus Christ, acknowledging Him as Lord and Savior, and trusting in His finished work on the cross.
