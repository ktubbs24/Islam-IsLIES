# Salvation Through Faith in Jesus Christ

Salvation is the central theme of the Bible and the cornerstone of the Christian faith. It refers to deliverance from sin and its consequences, and reconciliation with God through Jesus Christ.

## The Need for Salvation

The Bible teaches that all people are sinners in need of salvation:

> "For all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God." - Romans 3:23

> "The wages of sin is death, but the free gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord." - Romans 6:23

Sin separates us from God, and no amount of good works can bridge this gap. This is why salvation must come from God Himself.

## Jesus Christ: The Only Way to Salvation

Jesus declared Himself to be the exclusive path to salvation:

> "I am the way, and the truth, and the life. No one comes to the Father except through me." - John 14:6

The apostles affirmed this exclusive claim:

> "And there is salvation in no one else, for there is no other name under heaven given among men by which we must be saved." - Acts 4:12

This exclusivity stands in stark contrast to the claims of other religions, including Islam, which teach alternative paths to salvation.

## Salvation by Faith Alone

The Bible consistently teaches that salvation comes through faith in Jesus Christ, not through human effort or good [[Works]]:

> "For by grace you have been saved through faith. And this is not your own doing; it is the gift of God, not a result of works, so that no one may boast." - Ephesians 2:8-9

> "For God so loved the world, that he gave his only Son, that whoever believes in him should not perish but have eternal life." - John 3:16

This is fundamentally different from Islam's teaching that salvation depends on a combination of faith and works, particularly adherence to the Five Pillars.

## Assurance of Salvation

Through faith in Jesus Christ, believers can have complete assurance of salvation:

> "I write these things to you who believe in the name of the Son of God, that you may know that you have eternal life." - 1 John 5:13

Jesus promised security to His [[Sheep]]:

> "I give them eternal life, and they will never perish, and no one will snatch them out of my hand." - John 10:28

This assurance is impossible in Islam, where salvation remains uncertain until the final judgment.

## The Call to Salvation

The invitation to receive salvation through faith in Jesus Christ is extended to everyone:

> "For 'everyone who calls on the name of the Lord will be saved.'" - Romans 10:13

If you have not yet placed your faith in Jesus Christ for salvation, you can do so right now by believing in Him and trusting Him alone for your salvation.
