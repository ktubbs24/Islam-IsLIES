# Sheep - Jesus as the Good Shepherd

In the Bible, Jesus frequently uses the metaphor of sheep and shepherd to describe His relationship with His followers. This powerful imagery reveals much about the nature of salvation and the Christian life.

## Jesus' Own Words

Jesus explicitly identifies Himself as the Good Shepherd:

> "I am the good shepherd. The good shepherd lays down his life for the sheep." - John 10:11

> "I am the good shepherd. I know my own and my own know me, just as the Father knows me and I know the Father; and I lay down my life for the sheep." - John 10:14-15

This relationship between Jesus and His followers is personal and sacrificial. He knows each one by name and gave His life to save them.

## The Characteristics of Jesus' Sheep

Jesus describes those who belong to Him as His sheep:

> "My sheep hear my voice, and I know them, and they follow me. I give them eternal life, and they will never perish, and no one will snatch them out of my hand." - John 10:27-28

Those who truly belong to Jesus:
1. Hear His voice - They recognize and respond to His word
2. Are known by Him - They have a personal relationship with Jesus
3. Follow Him - Their lives reflect His teachings
4. Receive eternal life - They are saved through faith in Him
5. Are secure - Their salvation is guaranteed by Jesus Himself

## The Lost Sheep

Jesus also told parables about seeking the lost sheep, demonstrating God's heart for those who are spiritually lost:

> "What do you think? If a man has a hundred sheep, and one of them has gone astray, does he not leave the ninety-nine on the mountains and go in search of the one that went astray? And if he finds it, truly, I say to you, he rejoices over it more than over the ninety-nine that never went astray." - Matthew 18:12-13

This reveals God's intense desire to seek and save the lost, which is fulfilled through [[Jesus]] and His [[Salvation]] offered to all who believe.

## Contrast with Islam

In Islam, Allah is often portrayed as distant and unknowable. The relationship between Allah and his followers is more like that of a master and servant than a shepherd and sheep. The intimate, caring relationship that Jesus describes between Himself and His followers is unique to Christianity.

Through faith in Jesus as the Good Shepherd, we enter into this beautiful relationship of care, protection, and eternal security.
