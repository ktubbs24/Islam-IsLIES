# Understanding Islamic Teachings from a Christian Perspective

Islamic teachings present a fundamentally different worldview than what we find in the Bible. While Islam acknowledges Jesus as a prophet, it denies His divinity, death on the cross, and resurrection – the very foundations of Christian faith.

## The Quran vs. The Bible

The Quran, written approximately 600 years after Christ, contradicts the Bible in numerous ways:

1. **Jesus's Identity**: The Bible clearly teaches that Jesus is the Son of God (Matthew 3:17), while the Quran denies this (Surah 19:35).
2. **Salvation**: The Bible teaches salvation through faith in Jesus Christ (John 3:16), while Islam teaches salvation through good works and adherence to the Five Pillars.
3. **The Crucifixion**: The Bible details Jesus's crucifixion, while the Quran claims Jesus was not crucified (Surah 4:157).

## Common Misconceptions

Many Muslims have misconceptions about Christianity, often taught to them by Islamic leaders. These include:

- Christians worship three gods (misunderstanding of the Trinity)
- The Bible has been corrupted (despite overwhelming historical evidence to the contrary)
- Christians follow Paul instead of Jesus
