# The Truth About Islamic Teachings

In this article, we'll examine several key Islamic teachings and compare them with Biblical truth to help readers understand the fundamental contradictions between Islam and Christianity.

## The Nature of God

### Islamic View

The Islamic concept of Allah presents him as a solitary, indivisible deity who is completely transcendent and separate from his creation. The Quran emphasizes:

> "Say, 'He is Allah, [who is] One, Allah, the Eternal Refuge. He neither begets nor is born, Nor is there to Him any equivalent.'" (Surah 112:1-4)

This view explicitly rejects the Christian understanding of God as Triune and the deity of Jesus Christ.

### Biblical Truth

The Bible presents God as one in essence but existing eternally as three persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. This is evident in passages such as:

> "Go therefore and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit" (Matthew 28:19)

The relationship between the persons of the Trinity is one of perfect love and communion, which reflects God's relational nature.

## The Person of Jesus Christ

### Islamic View

In Islam, Jesus (Isa) is considered merely a prophet, not divine. The Quran states:

> "O People of the Scripture, do not commit excess in your religion or say about Allah except the truth. The Messiah, Jesus, the son of Mary, was but a messenger of Allah and His word which He directed to Mary and a soul [created at a command] from Him." (Surah 4:171)

Islam explicitly denies Jesus's crucifixion, claiming:

> "And they did not kill him, nor did they crucify him; but [another] was made to resemble him to them." (Surah 4:157)

### Biblical Truth

The Bible clearly teaches that Jesus is fully God and fully man:

> "In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God... And the Word became flesh and dwelt among us" (John 1:1, 14)

His crucifixion is the central event of Christianity, attested by all four Gospels and numerous historical sources.

## Salvation

### Islamic View

Islam teaches a salvation based on works, where good deeds are weighed against bad deeds:

> "Then those whose balance (of good deeds) is heavy, they will be successful. But those whose balance is light, will be those who have lost their souls, in Hell will they abide." (Surah 23:102-103)

### Biblical Truth

The Bible teaches salvation by grace through faith in Christ:

> "For by grace you have been saved through faith. And this is not your own doing; it is the gift of God, not a result of works, so that no one may boast." (Ephesians 2:8-9)

## Conclusion

The differences between Islamic teachings and Biblical Christianity are not merely cultural or superficial but relate to fundamental questions about the nature of God, the person of Christ, and the way of salvation. These differences cannot be reconciled without compromising the essential truths of one faith or the other.

Christians need to understand these differences clearly to effectively share the truth of the Gospel with Muslim friends and neighbors.
