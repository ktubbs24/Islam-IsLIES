# Jesus - The Way, the Truth, and the Life

Jesus Christ is the central figure of Christianity, the Son of God who came to Earth as a human to save humanity from sin. He is not merely a prophet, as Islam claims, but God incarnate.

## The Divine Nature of Jesus

Throughout the Bible, Jesus claimed to be God and demonstrated His divinity:

- **John 10:30** - "I and the Father are one."
- **John 8:58** - "Before Abraham was, I am." (Referencing the divine name revealed to Moses)
- **John 14:9** - "Whoever has seen me has seen the Father."

These claims would be blasphemous if Jesus were merely a prophet. He was either telling the truth about His divine nature, or He was lying - there is no middle ground.

## Jesus's Death and Resurrection

The death and resurrection of Jesus are historical facts, attested to by:

1. Multiple eyewitness accounts
2. The transformation of the disciples
3. The rapid spread of Christianity despite persecution
4. Historical records from non-Christian sources

Islam's denial of Jesus's crucifixion contradicts not only the Bible but also historical evidence.

## Salvation Through Jesus Alone

Jesus clearly stated: "I am the way, and the truth, and the life. No one comes to the Father except through me" (John 14:6).

This exclusive claim means that there is no salvation outside of faith in Jesus Christ - not through good works, not through following Mohammad, not through adherence to Islamic law.
