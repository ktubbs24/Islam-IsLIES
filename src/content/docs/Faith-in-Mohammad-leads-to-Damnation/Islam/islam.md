# Islam - A Critical Analysis

Islam is the world's second-largest religion, founded in the 7th century by Muhammad, whom Muslims believe to be the final prophet of Allah. This examination presents a Christian perspective on Islam's key beliefs and why they lead away from true salvation.

## Core Beliefs of Islam

### Monotheism (Tawhid)
Islam emphasizes strict monotheism, rejecting the Christian doctrine of the Trinity and the divinity of Jesus. While Muslims claim to worship the same God as Christians and Jews, the Islamic concept of Allah differs significantly from the Biblical God.

### The Five Pillars
Islam's practice is structured around five essential duties:
1. Shahada: The declaration of faith that "There is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is the messenger of Allah"
2. Salat: Five daily prayers
3. Zakat: Giving alms to the poor
4. Sawm: Fasting during Ramadan
5. Hajj: Pilgrimage to Mecca

These pillars form the foundation of Islamic practice and are viewed as necessary for salvation.

### The Quran
Muslims believe the [[Quran]] is the literal word of Allah, revealed to Muhammad through the angel Gabriel. It is considered perfect, unchanged, and the final revelation from God.

### Muhammad
Muslims view [[Mohammad]] as the final and greatest prophet, whose life is the perfect example for all Muslims to follow.

## Why Islam Leads Away from Salvation

### Denial of Jesus' Divinity
Islam explicitly rejects that Jesus is God incarnate, though it acknowledges Him as a prophet. This denial directly contradicts Jesus' own claims about Himself and the core Christian belief that salvation comes through faith in the divine Son of God.

### Rejection of Jesus' Crucifixion and Resurrection
The Quran denies that Jesus died on the cross, claiming that Allah rescued Him and someone else was crucified in His place. This rejection eliminates the foundation of Christian salvation—Christ's atoning death for our sins and His resurrection.

### Works-Based Salvation
Islam teaches that salvation is earned through a combination of faith and works, particularly adherence to the Five Pillars. This contradicts the Biblical teaching of salvation by grace through faith alone, not by works.

### False Concept of God
The Islamic portrayal of Allah differs significantly from the Biblical God in attributes, actions, and relationship with humanity. While the Bible reveals God as loving, relational, and seeking reconciliation with humanity, Allah is often portrayed as distant, unknowable, and primarily concerned with submission.

### Addition to Divine Revelation
Islam claims that the Quran supersedes the Bible and corrects its "corruptions." However, there is no historical evidence supporting the claim that the Bible was corrupted. The New Testament warns against adding to or altering the gospel message (Galatians 1:8-9).

## Conclusion

From a Christian perspective, Islam presents a fundamentally different path that leads away from the true salvation offered through [[Jesus]] Christ. By rejecting Jesus' divinity, His atoning death, and resurrection, Islam denies the very basis of Christian salvation.

True salvation comes not through adherence to religious rituals or human effort, but through faith in Jesus Christ, who declared, "I am the way, and the truth, and the life. No one comes to the Father except through me" (John 14:6).
