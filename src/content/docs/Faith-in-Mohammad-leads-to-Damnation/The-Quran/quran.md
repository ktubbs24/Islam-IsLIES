# The Quran - A Critical Examination

The Quran is considered by Muslims to be the literal word of Allah, revealed to Muhammad over a 23-year period in the 7th century CE. This analysis examines the Quran from a Christian perspective, highlighting its inconsistencies with Biblical truth.

## Historical Background

The Quran was allegedly revealed to Muhammad through the angel Gabriel between 610 and 632 CE. Unlike the Bible, which was written by multiple authors over 1,500 years with remarkable consistency, the Quran was narrated by a single individual and compiled after his death.

Muhammad initially could not distinguish whether the spirit that appeared to him was divine or demonic, and even contemplated suicide due to his confusion. This raises serious questions about the source of these revelations.

## Contradictions with History and Science

### Historical Inaccuracies
The Quran contains numerous historical errors, including:

- Confusing Mary (mother of Jesus) with Miriam (sister of Moses and Aaron), despite them living 1,500 years apart (Surah 19:27-28)
- Claiming that Alexander the Great (Dhul-Qarnayn) was a Muslim who lived to an old age, when historical records show he was a polytheist who died at age 32 (Surah 18:83-98)
- Stating that crucifixion was used in ancient Egypt during Joseph's time, centuries before this method of execution was invented (Surah 12:41)

### Scientific Problems
While some claim the Quran contains scientific miracles, it actually includes numerous scientific errors:

- Describing the sun setting in a muddy spring (Surah 18:86)
- Claiming that human embryos develop from "a clot of blood" (Surah 96:1-2)
- Stating that the sky is a "roof" that could fall on earth (Surah 21:32)

## Theological Contradictions

### Internal Contradictions
The Quran contradicts itself on numerous points, including:

- Whether Allah's words can be changed (Compare Surah 6:115 with Surah 2:106)
- Whether compulsion in religion is allowed (Compare Surah 2:256 with Surah 9:5,29)
- Whether Allah forgives shirk (associating partners with Allah) (Compare Surah 4:48 with Surah 4:153)

### Contradictions with the Bible
The Quran claims to confirm the Bible (Surah 5:48) yet contradicts it on fundamental points:

- It denies Jesus' divinity, despite Jesus' own claims to be God (John 8:58, 10:30)
- It denies Jesus' crucifixion (Surah 4:157), a historical event attested by Christian and non-Christian sources
- It presents a different version of many Biblical stories, often with historical inaccuracies

## The Preservation Question

Muslims claim the Quran has been perfectly preserved, but historical evidence shows:

- Multiple variant readings existed early in Islamic history
- Uthman ordered all variant Qurans burned and established one authorized version
- Early manuscripts like the Sana'a manuscripts show textual variations

## The Ethical Problem

The Quran and Hadith contain passages that raise serious ethical concerns:

- Permitting men to beat their wives (Surah 4:34)
- Allowing men to marry prepubescent girls, based on Muhammad's marriage to Aisha
- Endorsing warfare against non-Muslims (Surah 9:5,29)
- Permitting sex with female captives of war (Surah 4:24)

## Conclusion

From a Christian perspective, the Quran cannot be considered divine revelation due to its historical inaccuracies, internal contradictions, scientific errors, and ethical problems. It contradicts the Bible on fundamental points while claiming to confirm it.

The Bible warns against accepting different gospels: "But even if we or an angel from heaven should preach to you a gospel contrary to the one we preached to you, let him be accursed" (Galatians 1:8).

True salvation comes through [[Jesus]] Christ alone, not through following the teachings of [[Mohammad]] or the [[Quran]].
